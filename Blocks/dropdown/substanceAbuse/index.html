Substance Abuse

Substance abuse refers to the harmful or hazardous use of psychoactive substances, including alcohol and illicit drugs. One of the key impacts of illicit drug use on society is the negative health consequences experienced by its members. Drug use also puts a heavy financial burden on individuals, families and society.

Understanding Addiction

Addiction is a chronic condition that affects the brain, leading to compulsive behaviours despite harmful consequences.

Commonly Abused Substances

Alcohol, druds, nicotine, and prescription medications are among the most frequenty misused substances.

Signs and Symptoms

Warning signs of addiction includes, changes in behaviour, physical health, and an inability to control use.

Treatment and recovery

Recovery is possible through therapy ,support groups, and medical inventions tailored to individal needs.

Prevention and education

awareness and education empower individuals to make informed choices and prevent addiction
