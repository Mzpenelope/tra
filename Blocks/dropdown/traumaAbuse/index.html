Trauma and Abuse

Trauma can be a normal response to abuse, and the effects can last into adulthood.

Trauma is a psychological injury that can be caused by an event or events that overwhelm a person's ability to cope, while, Abuse is when someone fails to respect another person's rights and dignity

examples of trauma :Natural disasters, witnessing or being involved in an accident, fighting in a war, being betrayed, abused, neglected, bullied, exploited, or assaulted. Psychological abuse, which is when one person behaves in a violent, demeaning, or invasive manner towards another person

Types of trauma

Trauma can be physical, emotional, phychological, or complex,stemming from events likr abuse, accidents, or loss.

short-term Effects

Inital effects of trauma may include shock, anxiety fear and difficulty concerntrating

long-term Effects

Untreated trauma can lead to chronic stress, depression, PTSD, and relationship challenges.

Healing and recovery

recoveryfrom trauma involves therapy,self-care, and gradually reclaiming a sense of safety.

Support systems

Strong support systems provide understanding ,encouragement , and a safe space to heal.
