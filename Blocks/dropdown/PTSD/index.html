Postpartum Depression (PPD) and Anxiety (PPA)

Postpartum depression (PPD) and postpartum anxiety (PPA) are both mental health issues that can affect new mothers:

PPD Symptoms include depression, crying, and difficulty bonding with your baby. PPD can also cause mood swings, insomnia, and feelings of guilt, worthlessness, or shame. PPD can start within the first few weeks after giving birth, but it can also begin during pregnancy or up to a year later.

PPA Symptoms include excessive worrying, panic, and feeling overwhelmed. PPA can occur by itself or in combination with PPD. While some signs of PPD and PPA overlap, such as disrupted sleep and feeling afraid, it's important to talk to a healthcare provider about all of your symptoms. Treatments for PPD and PPA include: Prescription medicine, Therapy with a mental health specialist, Cognitive behavioral therapy, Mindfulness techniques, and Online or in-person support groups.
